About

Dr. Jie Yang is a researcher at the Advanced Neural Chip Center, Westlake University, and the Zhejiang Provincial Engineering Research Center for BCI Intelligent Chip Systems. He received his Ph.D. in Microelectronics from the Institute of Semiconductors, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and completed his postdoctoral training at the University of Calgary, Canada.

Dr. Yang’s research focuses on brain-computer interfaces (BCI) and neuromorphic (brain-inspired) computing chips, including high-throughput BCI chips, closed-loop neural modulation systems, neuromorphic computing, and brain-language decoding. He has led the design of over 20 advanced chips and published more than 50 papers in leading journals and conferences, including JSSC, TBioCAS, TBME, JNE, JBHI, ISSCC, ECCV, and ICLR.

He is a recipient of several talent awards, including the West Lake Pearl Project and Zhejiang Provincial High-level Talent. Dr. Yang currently leads major national and provincial research projects with total funding exceeding 20 million RMB, driving innovation in BCI and neuromorphic technology from theory to real-world applications.

Research Interests

My research aims to protect, connect, and simulate the brain, focusing on neural modulation, brain-computer interfaces, and neuromorphic computing.

Protecting the Brain: We develop closed-loop neural modulation chips that combine real-time brain signal acquisition, AI processing, and feedback stimulation, enabling precise diagnosis and treatment of neurological disorders such as autism, epilepsy, depression, Parkinson’s, and Alzheimer’s disease.

Connecting the Brain: Our high-throughput, low-power BCI chips support thousands of channels for neural signal acquisition and wireless transmission. With advanced decoding algorithms, these systems enable applications in speech restoration, rehabilitation, and intention recognition.

Simulating the Brain: Inspired by the brain’s efficiency, we design neuromorphic chips with low power and lifelong learning capabilities. These chips are used in intelligent sensing, robotics, and biomedical devices, offering significant improvements in energy efficiency over traditional processors.

Teaching & Books

Dr. Yang has taught and co-taught courses such as "Integrated Micro and Nanotechnology Sensory Systems" (University of Calgary), "Digital Circuits," "Hardware Description Language and System Simulation," and "Integrated Circuits Design" (Westlake University).

He is the lead author of "Invasive Brain-Computer Interface Chip Design" (featured in the national textbook "Brain-Computer Interface") and co-author of the English monograph "Emerging Trends of Biomedical Circuits and Systems" with Prof. Sawan. Dr. Yang is also co-leading a curriculum reform project on interdisciplinary integrated circuit education, with open-source teaching materials to be released for the academic community.
